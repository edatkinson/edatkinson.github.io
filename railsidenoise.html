Numerical Investigation into Railside Noise Barriers

This project involved building a numerical model to simulate wave propagation using partial differential equations (PDEs). The goal was to evaluate various materials for their effectiveness in attenuating sound:

  • Wave propagation simulation: PDEs were used to model how sound waves travel through different media.
  • Material testing: Different materials were tested to determine their sound attenuation properties.
  • Optimization: The most effective materials were identified for use in railside noise barriers, potentially reducing noise pollution in urban areas.

This investigation provided valuable insights into the design of more effective noise barriers, which could have significant applications in both urban planning and environmental management.
