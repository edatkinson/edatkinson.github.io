Optimising Aviva’s Garage Network

This group project focused on optimising the placement of Aviva’s repair garages to reduce tow truck drive times, enhance sustainability, and ensure efficient emergency assistance. The project involved the use of data-driven approaches and mathematical modeling to address the challenge of strategically positioning repair garages across the UK.

  • Garage Location Optimisation Using K-Means Clustering: We used a weighted K-Means clustering algorithm to optimise the placement of garages based on collision data. This method helped to reduce the average distance between collisions and the nearest garage.
  • Community Detection: Utilised the Louvain community detection method to identify clusters within the network and reposition garages to efficiently manage repair demands across these communities.
  • Distance Metrics: Employed Dijkstra’s Algorithm to calculate the shortest paths from garages to collision sites, allowing us to assess the impact of our optimisation strategies on response times.
  • Statistical Analysis: Conducted comparative analysis between the initial and optimised networks, showing that the K-Means method significantly reduced mean distances and variance in drive times, making it the superior optimisation technique.

This project demonstrated that optimising the location of garages can lead to improved operational efficiency and sustainability for Aviva. The reduction in drive times not only enhances customer satisfaction but also contributes to lowering CO2 emissions.
